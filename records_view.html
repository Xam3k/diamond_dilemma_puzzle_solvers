Diamond Dilemma (1988) — best known results

The gold challenge of the Diamond Dilemma puzzle — place all 160 tiles on the pentagonal bipyramid so the gold lines form one closed loop — remains unsolved. These are the strongest known partial results, produced by the solvers in this repository. Tile data and the published silver/red/blue solutions used to validate our entire pipeline are due to Jaap Scherphuis — thank you, Jaap.

Category A — most tiles with every touching edge matched: 142/160

All 240 gold half-edges between placed tiles match perfectly; the 18 hatched slots are holes no remaining tile fits (each was proven "dead" — see NEGATIVE_RESULTS.md). Robust against 120 perturbation kicks and a 102-slot coordinated re-solve.

Category B — most matched edges with all 160 tiles placed: 208/240

Eternity-II-style score: every tile is on the board; 32 edges (red) do not match. Produced by CP-SAT large-neighbourhood search (rr_edges.py); survived ~1,600 provably-optimal 52–64-tile rearrangements and ten degrade-reclimb restarts without improvement.
